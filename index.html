Protecting Young Children in the Digital Age

A Growing Concern

The Issue

The digital age reshapes childhood, offering convenience for parents and educators, but simultaneously raises major concerns about its affects on young children's development, health, and behavior

Key information

  • "Excessive screen time" refers to usage beyond the American Academy of Pediatrics' (AAP) Recommendations.
  • 8 year olds spend up to 6 hours a day on screen time, triple their recommended amount.
  • Infants, who are not recommended any screen time, are averaging 56 minutes per day.

    • The Hidden Costs

    Impact on Health

  • Prolonged sedentary behavior, increasing obesity and poor diet.
  • Increased vision issues
  • Higher rates of depression and overlall lower quality of life

    • Developmental Impact

  • Emotional: Impairs regulation, leading to irritability
  • Cognitive: Compromises functioning, causing difficulties in following directions and task completion
  • Social: Diminishes crucial parent-child interaction.
  • Physical: Reduces opportunities for vigorous activity.

    • Behavioral Challenges

  • Children who use screens more than two hours a day are more likely to exhibit inattention, aggression, and hyperactivity.
  • Show signs of frustration and lack of emotional regulation.
  • Reveal a desensitization to violence due to viewing inappropriate content.

    • Addressing Common Misconceptions

    It's Educational!

    An ongoing debate: Using screen time is learning time- so it’s ok for it to be in excess.

  • In reality, children under the age of three-years-old do not learn from digital content. Research shows that young children do not relate what is shown on-screen to real life, therefore it is irrelevant and purely entertainment.
  • Older children (3 - 8 years old) can learn effectively from a screen, but should still follow the recommended guidelines for screen use. Additionally, children learn better from technology when there is an adult working along with them.
  • Ultimately, nothing will replace face-to-face instruction or learning through play.

    • There’s No Time / Resources!

    A difficult issue which elevates screen usage: Parents and educators are overworked and lack additional entertainment and teaching materials.

    Here are some starting actions

  • Get outside: enhance adventure.
  • Embrace Boredom: allow children to self-entertain.
  • Get in touch: Community help and resources are available.
